Radiology.

Radiology is a medical field utilizing imaging techniques to diagnose and treat conditions within the body. It relies on principles of using radiation or sound waves to create images, distinguishing between healthy and diseased tissues based on density and composition. Key modalities include X-ray, CT, MRI, ultrasound, and nuclear medicine, each with unique applications. Recent technological advancements, such as digital radiography, 3D imaging, AI integration, and advanced contrast agents, have enhanced diagnostic accuracy and patient care. Radiology remains indispensable in modern healthcare, enabling early disease detection and precise treatment.
